What I'd Build at Wave

The agent is the product.
Fix their world first.

"Wave's 150,000 agents aren't a distribution channel — they are the product. The moment their experience becomes seamless, client trust and growth follow as a natural consequence."

Across Senegal, Côte d'Ivoire, and Uganda, most users' experience of Wave is mediated through an agent — not the app. An agent who struggles to onboard, gets confused by a new feature rollout, or loses a transaction because of a workflow gap doesn't just lose that transaction. They erode the client's trust in Wave entirely.

I've lived this problem directly. At One Acre Fund, our shop agents had a 4.7/10 trust score in our POS system. Transactions failed daily. The instinct was to blame the technology — but the real issue was simpler: we had never understood what the agent's day actually looked like. The friction, the workarounds, the moments of pressure where mistakes crept in. When we fixed that, everything else improved.

That is exactly where I'd start at Wave.

01
Deep Agent Discovery
Run structured user discovery sessions across Wave's key markets. Map the full agent workflow end-to-end: onboarding friction, new feature adoption gaps, transaction failure points, and the exact moments where fraud finds its opening. Not a survey — observation, interviews, and ride-alongs in the field.
02
Frictionless Onboarding & Feature Rollout
New agents and new features should feel effortless to absorb. I'd define an onboarding flow and in-app training experience that gets agents productive fast — closing the window of uncertainty where mistakes and fraud are most likely.
03
Close Fraud Gaps at the Workflow Level
Fraud doesn't happen in the system — it happens in the gaps between steps, when an agent is rushed, confused, or outside of their normal flow. I'd map those friction points from discovery data and reduce the opportunity surface for fraud through smarter UX, not just back-end detection.
04
Measure Trust — Not Just Transactions
Define an agent satisfaction and client trust metric that feeds directly into expansion decisions. If clients love their agent and the experience is seamless, growth into new markets and new products becomes a natural consequence of the foundation you've built.
The Outcome
Agents who are confident, well-equipped, and operating with minimal friction become Wave's most powerful growth engine. Client trust in Wave is built one agent interaction at a time. Get that right, and expansion into Cameroon, into new demographics, into savings and credit products — becomes a natural byproduct of the foundation you've earned.

🇰🇪 Rural Kenya — Shop Agent Visits
Watching transactions fail under real pressure
Spent days at Tupande retail shops watching agents process M-Pesa transactions during peak harvest season. Saw exactly where the POS failed — timeout errors, unconfirmed payment states, confusion on retry flows. Built the new UX from those observations, not assumptions.
🌾 Farmer Interviews — Input Ordering
How farmers actually think about digital money
Many of our farmers had never used e-commerce. I interviewed them directly about trust, payment anxiety, and what "confirmation" meant to them. This reshaped our order confirmation UX — SMS fallbacks, simplified receipts — all from what I heard in the field.
📊 197-Person Survey — POS Reliability
Quantifying a trust crisis no one had named
When agents complained, leadership assumed minor friction. I ran a structured 197-person survey across Kenya and found a 4.7/10 trust score and widespread failures. That data became the business case for a full rebuild and an engineering warroom before peak season.
